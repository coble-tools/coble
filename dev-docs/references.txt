journal=Nature protocols
year=2024
citations=18 (!)
link=https://www.nature.com/articles/s41596-024-00986-0
readable link=https://arxiv.org/pdf/2203.16261
The readable link has a slightly different title but I think it is basically the same paper just not behind a paywall. 
"The ability of many independent researchers to install and successfully run omics
software tools supports scientific integrity by ensuring transparency and reproducibility of
research results"
And even more key to what COBLE targets:
"However, omics software tools often fail when reproduction is attempted because of the many challenges 
associated with their installation and use. Some such common challenges include the absence of standardized
interfaces, installation procedures, and data formats; a user’s limited computational skills;
missing dependencies; different prerequisites and restrictions for various pieces of software;
operating system (OS) incompatibility; and lack of user permissions on the host computer."
They compare 3 approaches: packaging, virtualisation and contanerisation. FOr packages they use the term "dependency hell".
They evidently favour containerisation. They have no solution but say that 
the inclusion of source code in methods doesn't help when people can't run it.
"Scientific journals have introduced rigorous requirements for the sharing of data and
source code; however, there are currently no effective requirements for the installability of
published software tools and the reproducibility of results based on such tools"

- Krafczyk, M. S., Shi, A., Bhaskar, A., Marinov, D., & Stodden, V. (2021). 
Learning from reproducing computational results: Introducing three principles and the Reproduction Package. Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A: Mathematical, Physical and Engineering Sciences, 379(2197), rsta.2020.0069, 20200069. https://doi.org/10.1098/rsta.2020.0069
Journal= Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society
year=2021
Citations=41 
link=https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rsta/article/379/2197/20200069/111842/Learning-from-reproducing-computational-results
Attempt to reproduce resiults from 7 published artciles. And derive 3 principles as a result.
There is a definition of reprodicible given.
Recommendation is to make the results deterministic, ie versions.
Recommendation to declare software dependencies and their versions. Notes that things evolve.
They recommend to package into docker or singularity. And or use conda or spack.
And or use a build system. ie clear instructions on how software is built. COBLE is I guess also a kind of build system.
They say to disclose resource requirements. And report the hardware used.
They say if possible include a small test case so people know it works - this I repeat is something I think we should enforce.
They say that following their recommended guidance has costs.
Note for COBLE it is the opposite, it is easy to build an environment and once you have done so you can not worry about losing it!

- Pelot, N. A., Wang, B., Marshall, D., Hussain, M., Musselman, E. D., Yu, G. J., Dale, J., Baumgart, I. W., Dardani, D., Zamani, P. T., Villacreses, D. F. C., Wagenaar, J., & Grill, W. M. (2025). 
Guidance for sharing computational models of neural stimulation: From project planning to publication. Journal of Neural Engineering. https://doi.org/10.1088/1741-2552/ADB997
Journal=Journal of neural Engineering
Year=2025
citations=3
link=https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1741-2552/adb997
This was surprisingly interesting. It highlights the 6 R's of reproducibility:
Re-runnability, Repeatability, Replicability, Reusability, Reprodicibity, Readability
It is about sharing computational models but the principles can extend to an environment to some extent.
Specifically the idea that the black box model of reproidicibility is not the goal, pressing a button and getting the same thing out.
But rather building something that can be used by others and expanded on. Such as a COBLE environment.
"reusability enables the scientific community to further develop a shared model: new research questions 
can be investigated while building from the same mathematical and theoretical foundation to maintain scientific continuity"
There is often talk of cost of planning to share, that literally
only 1 environment is ever built then peope are surpised it is hard to build again.
This comes back to also disaster recovery, the cost of not being able to recreate is high.
